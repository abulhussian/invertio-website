What is a Digital Transformation Strategy?

A digital transformation strategy helps in planning, introducing, analyzing, and driving a digital transformation initiative. The process will help define goals to achieve digital transformation.

With an effective digital transformation strategy, you’ll create a framework and follow an ever-evolving process. But before you begin, you should know what you are hoping to achieve that helps designate KPIs to track.

You probably require high-level metrics for measuring adoption: log-in rates usage, data quality, and business performance.

In this case, you might drill down further and monitor metrics such as:

  • Monthly sales volumes
  • Opportunities created
  • Sales productivity (e.g., time saved, the volume of sales activity per rep)
  • User log-in rates
  • Prospect account with key fields populated

A well-thought-out strategy helps address what kind of effect it will have on customers and employees. Announcing and supporting the transformation will help generate momentum. Regular feedback from various resources also keeps the ball rolling.

How to Measure ROI on Digital Transformation?

Digital transformations require investments of both time and money, so it’s essential to have a system in place to measure the return on investment (ROI).

To establish how you’ll measure and track your digital transformation’s success, follow these three steps: A digital transformation consulting firm can go a long way ahead in meeting ROI goals.

1. Set goals — What does success mean to you? What is your ideal outcome?

2. Establish Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) — What metrics will you monitor? How will those metrics tell the story of your digital transformation? What level of KPI growth are you hoping to achieve?

3. Monitor progress — What types of reports will you use to keep an eye on your KPIs and the progress of your transformation? How often will you run reports and manage strategies?

To illustrate how a business might apply these steps in a real-world scenario, here’s a simple example:

Goal: Decrease customer support queries without negatively affecting customer satisfaction

KPIs: Number of support queries received each day, time spent responding to support tickets, average support query response time

Monitor: Run weekly reports on KPIs and send monthly surveys to customers to gauge satisfaction
