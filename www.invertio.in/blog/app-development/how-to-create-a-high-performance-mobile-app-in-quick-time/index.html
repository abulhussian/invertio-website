A Complete Breakdown Of Phases: Timeline For Developing High-Performing Mobile Apps

Building a high-performance mobile app can take a few weeks to several months. The timeline also depends on the app’s intricacy and the developer’s skillset. Sticking to a precise timeline and smooth coordination with the development team is imperative if you want your business app to meet the target market’s requirements and function flawlessly across different mobile devices.

Here is an overview of all the phases of app development within defined timeline:

Discovery phase: Research the target audience to comprehend their requirements and inspect the competition. The duration of this phase can vary from one to two weeks, depending on the project’s complexity.

Design phase: This phase involves crafting the app’s wireframes, UI and UX. It may require two to three weeks, depending on the intricacy of the project.

Development phase: Entails building the app’s core features, incorporating APIs, and assessing its performance. The step may require a month to two months.

Testing phase: It involves testing the app on mobile devices and OSs. The testing duration may take one to two weeks, depending on the intricacy of the project.

Deployment phase: The final step is to submit the application to the app store and make it accessible to the public. The process should not take more than a week.

Maintenance phase: Post-launch requires continuous maintenance to mend issues, add functions, and improve performance. This will be an ongoing stage.

Building High-performance Apps In Less Time: Steps to Follow

“How to make a successful app in less time”?

Businesses often find it challenging to make a high-performance mobile app quickly. The steps given below will help you develop an app in less time. 

Let’s have a look at the app development tips for creating successful apps:

Step 1: Define Your Goals & Objectives

The first step is critical in “how to make a successful app,” as it involves specific goals and achievements you want to accomplish. For this, you need to follow some sub-steps as mentioned below:

  • Identify the purpose of the app
  • Determine the scope of the project
  • Set measurable goals and objectives
  • Conduct feasibility analysis
  • Define the development timeline

If you follow the above steps correctly, you can properly define your goals and objective. It will further help you:

Get a clear understanding of your achievements: Setting clear, measurable goals and objectives can help define what needs to be achieved and create a plan of action to reach them.

Measure your progress: Having clear objectives allows you to assess your progress in achieving your aims, enabling you to modify your approach as necessary.

Make you stay focused: When you possess concrete aims and objectives, you are more likely to remain undistracted and prevent anything that could slow down your progress.

Make better decisions: Establishing clear goals and objectives allows you to make informed decisions by gauging which option best aligns with the desired results.

Step 2: Plan Your App Architecture

This step involves designing the overall structure of the smooth app, including its modules, components, and how they interact. The architecture provides a high-level view of the different parts of the application for the development team. 

It also ensures the applications are built with maintainability, scalability, and extensibility in mind. Furthermore, it helps reduce development time by avoiding architectural issues in the development process.

This includes:

  • Data model creation: It is essential to determine the arrangement for storing and obtaining data in the application, incorporating the database schema and connections between data elements.
  • Defining the components: It is essential to identify the different components of an application, such as the user interface, data repository, and programming logic, and understand how they each serve a unique purpose.
  • Application’s architecture pattern: Opting for a design pattern that fulfills the application’s needs is an integral step. Examples of these are the Model-View-Controller pattern or the microservices architecture.

  • Application’s flow plan: When an application is given user input or subjected to system events, one must establish the necessary steps for a response.
  • Identifying integration points: Integrating external systems or services is an essential step for the application, including payment gateways, social media networks, or any third-party APIs.

Step 3: Choose The Right Development Approach

Making an application with the most appropriate approach or methodology for the development process based on team structure, project requirements, and other factors is an important step. This approach depends on several factors, such as the project’s complexity, the development team’s size, and the budget and timeline constraints.  

For the best apps to create, there are several development approaches to choose from, including:

Waterfall: The development process follows a linear and orderly fashion, wherein each stage must be concluded before moving on to the next.

Agile: This approach optimizes the application’s progress through iterative flexibility, development sprints, and ongoing evaluation,

Hybrid: It is a combination of both the waterfall and agile methodologies.

DevOps: The synergy between the development and operations teams are strengthened through this method. Ultimately, it leads to efficient, reliable creation, testing, and application release.

Lean: This methodology prioritizes value maximization and waste reduction in development. The ultimate goal is to deliver the greatest possible outcome by recognizing and discarding any unhelpful practices to the product’s outcome.

Step 4: Choose The Right Technology Stack

Using the right tools and technologies to streamline the development process and develop a high-performance app in less time is essential. You can select the most appropriate combination of technologies, frameworks, and tools based on your project’s requirements and team expertise.

Here are some pointers to consider while choosing a technology stack for your business app:

Choose the right programming language: Choose a language well-suited for the app’s requirements and your development team’s skillset.

Use a reliable integrated development environment (IDE): An IDE can help you streamline development by providing code editing, debugging, and testing tools.

Utilize open-source libraries and frameworks: Open-source libraries and frameworks can help you speed up development by providing pre-built code and functionalities.

Use cloud services and platforms: Mobile app development services, including cloud services and platforms, can help reduce infrastructure costs and improve scalability.

Step 5: Test & Debug Your App

This step involves systematically checking the application to find and fix issues or bugs immediately. It ensures good app performance and meets the user’s expectations.

Here are some pointers to consider while testing & debugging:

Conduct comprehensive testing: Thorough testing helps detect malfunctions, mistakes, or app performance problems.

Optimize the app’s performance: Use performance monitoring tools to pinpoint potential problems and fine-tune the output.

Get user feedback: Try to gather individual input to pinpoint any issues or areas needing improvement.

Step 6: Launch & Market Your App

Launching and marketing the developed app is the final step in developing high-performance apps in less time. It can increase your app’s visibility and reach a wider audience.

Here are some pointers to consider in this stage:

Set a launch date: Establish an official release date for the app, and ensure it fulfills all prerequisites, including the submission to app stores.

Develop a marketing plan: A good marketing strategy is essential to advertise the app to its intended audience effectively. Incorporating tactics such as social media outreach, content generation, and email campaigns, we can incorporate paid promotion for successful outreach.

Gather user feedback: After the release, collecting user feedback can help recognize any problems or possibilities for advancement. Doing this will not only help in optimizing the app but also in sustaining customers.

Analyze app performance: App analytics tools help track your app’s success, thus granting you the insight necessary to make decisions that will help elevate performance in the long run.
